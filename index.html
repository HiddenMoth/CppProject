A digital final project exploring authorship, multimodality, and the nature of writing.

What is Writing?

Writing is traditionally understood as the act of inscribing language into a physical or digital form. However, in contemporary literacy studies, writing extends beyond alphabetic text. It encompasses multimodal communication: images, sounds, digital interfaces, gestures, typography, and code.

In this course, we explored how writing is not only a tool for communication but also a process of meaning-making, invention, interpretation, and representation. Writing is both material and conceptual: it produces ideas, identities, and social relationships.

Who is a Writer?

A writer is not defined solely by profession or mastery but by participation in acts of composing. Anyone who engages in shaping meaning—through words, images, digital media, gestures, or design—is practicing writing.

In this expanded framework, writers include:

  • Students composing academic essays
  • Designers building visual narratives
  • Coders writing interactive media
  • Communities creating shared texts
  • Digital tools that support or transform expression

Writing is an activity that emerges through relationships between people, tools, language, materials, and contexts.
